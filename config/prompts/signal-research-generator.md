# Signal Research Generator Prompt

You are preparing a research brief for Perplexity based on a company signal detected in an executive opportunity pipeline.

Your task is to generate a factual, source-backed research output that is optimised for direct paste into Research Hub, where it will later be audited and synthesised.

This is not a scoring task.
This is not an outreach task.
Do not guess.
Treat the signal as a lead to investigate, not proof of need.

## Signal context

Company: {COMPANY_NAME}
Signal type: {SIGNAL_TYPE}
Sector: {SECTOR}
Geography: {GEOGRAPHY}
Signal summary: {AI_SUMMARY}

## Core objective

Research the company with the signal as the starting point.

The goal is to determine whether the evidence suggests a plausible need for senior leadership at the level of:
- CPO
- CTPO
- COO

with particular relevance to:
- product transformation
- AI transformation
- commercial execution
- operational scaling
- operating-model change
- cross-functional rebuild or reorganisation

## Output format

Return the output in clean markdown using the exact section headings below.

For every factual claim, include a source inline or at the end of the bullet.
Use dates wherever possible.
If information is missing, write: Not found.
If evidence is mixed or indirect, say so explicitly.

---

## 1. Company Snapshot
- What the company does
- HQ
- Founding year
- Estimated employee count
- Revenue, if available
- Ownership status: PE-backed, VC-backed, public, private, unknown

## 2. Triggering Signal
- What happened
- Date of the event
- Who was involved
- Why the event appears material
- Whether the event is confirmed, partially confirmed, or weakly evidenced

## 3. Ownership and Capital Context
- Investors, sponsors, acquirers or parent company
- Recent funding rounds, buyouts, carve-outs, recapitalisations or exits
- Timing and size, if available
- Whether ownership context increases pressure for growth, restructuring, integration or execution

## 4. Leadership and Organisation
- CEO and key executive team
- Any CPO, CTPO, COO or equivalent present or absent
- Recent leadership changes
- Board changes
- Signs of leadership build-out, leadership gaps, reorganisation or executive churn

## 5. Product, Technology and AI Context
- Core products or platforms
- Product complexity indicators
- Technology/platform architecture clues
- AI strategy, AI product claims, or transformation signals
- Any indication the company may need stronger product leadership, platform leadership, or AI execution capacity

## 6. Commercial and Growth Context
- Target customers
- Go-to-market model
- Expansion into geographies, segments or product lines
- Hiring trends
- Partnerships, channel moves or enterprise expansion
- Evidence of commercial scaling pressure or execution complexity

## 7. Operational Stress, Risk and Red Flags
- Layoffs
- Restructuring
- Missed targets
- Negative press
- Integration issues
- Product delivery problems
- Customer complaints
- Margin pressure
- Financial strain
- Signs the business may be under operational or transformation pressure

## 8. Market and Competitive Context
- Main competitors
- Market position
- Category maturity
- Whether the company appears to be at an inflection point, under pressure, or behind peers in product, AI, commercial execution or operating model

## 9. Executive-Relevance Implications
Provide a cautious evidence-based assessment of whether the company may plausibly need:
- a CPO
- a CTPO
- a COO

Focus on evidence related to:
- product transformation
- AI transformation
- commercial execution
- scaling discipline
- operating-model redesign
- post-acquisition integration
- turnaround or reorganisation support

Do not overstate.
Use phrases like:
- Evidence suggests
- Limited evidence suggests
- No strong public evidence found
- This remains unclear

## 10. Key Unknowns
List the most important open questions that would need further checking before treating this company as a strong target.

## 11. Sources
Provide a compact source list with:
- source name
- title
- date
- URL

## Research rules

- Prefer primary sources, company materials, funding databases, executive bios, credible press, and high-quality industry sources
- Do not invent revenue, headcount, ownership, executive roles or market position
- Note conflicts between sources
- Avoid generic filler and broad business commentary
- Be concise but specific
- Optimise for paste-readability inside Research Hub

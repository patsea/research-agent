User Guide

How to use the Job Search Agent system to find, research, score, and outreach to executive opportunities.

What This System Does

This is a local AI agent pipeline that automates the research and outreach process for executive job opportunities. It monitors signals (job postings, portfolio moves, fund raises), researches companies and contacts, scores opportunities, finds contact details, drafts personalised outreach emails, and manages the Gmail draft queue.

All agents run locally on your Mac. No data leaves your machine except for API calls to Anthropic (Claude), FullEnrich, and Perplexity. All email output goes to Gmail Drafts — nothing is ever sent automatically.

Key principle: The system surfaces opportunities and drafts outreach. You review and send every email. The agents do the research and writing; you make every decision.

The Agents

Signal Scanner
localhost:3033
Monitors job boards, fund websites, LinkedIn, and news for CPO/COO/Operating Partner signals. Produces a scored list of opportunities. Runs on a configurable schedule.
Research Hub
localhost:3035
Deep research on a company or fund. Uses Perplexity and Claude to generate a brief on the company, investment thesis, portfolio, and likely fit. Also runs interview prep and ELNS (portfolio company assessment).
Scorer
localhost:3038
Takes a company or opportunity and scores it against your target profile: fund stage, geography, sector, role type, and investment thesis alignment. Returns a 0–100 score with reasoning.
Contact Research
localhost:3036
Given a company or fund name, finds the right contact — Operating Partner, Talent Partner, or Managing Partner — and enriches their email via FullEnrich. Returns verified contact details ready for outreach.
Outreach Drafter
localhost:3037
The core drafting agent. Takes a contact, company, campaign type, and optional research context, and generates a personalised outreach email using your POSITIONING.md as the source of truth. Supports rewrite buttons and full regeneration.
Email Scan
localhost:3034
Scans your Gmail inbox for replies, OOO messages, bounces, and new opportunities. Creates Attio tasks for follow-ups. Can also detect inbound signals from contacts who have opened or replied to outreach.

Gmail Hygiene Port 3039

Scans your inbox, classifies senders, and manages unsubscribes and spam. API-only — no UI.

What it does

  • Scan the last N days of your inbox
  • Classify senders by type (newsletter, marketing, transactional, personal)
  • Identify unsubscribe candidates based on frequency and engagement
  • Never auto-blocks — you confirm every unsubscribe and block action

Key endpoints

  • POST /api/scanScan inbox for the last N days (body: {"days": 30})
  • GET /api/sendersList all classified senders
  • POST /api/senders/:id/unsubscribeUnsubscribe from a sender
  • POST /api/senders/:id/blockBlock a sender
  • GET /api/healthHealth check

First-time setup

Run a 30-day scan to build the sender database:

curl -X POST http://localhost:3039/api/scan -H "Content-Type: application/json" -d '{"days": 30}'

Cold start timeout: The first npx invocation may take 30-60 seconds to install dependencies. Subsequent calls are fast. If the scan times out, just retry.

Step-by-Step Workflow

A. Full Pipeline Run (new opportunity)

  1. Open the Dashboard at localhost:3030
    All agents should show green dots in the sidebar. If any are red, they are not running — start them from the terminal.
  2. Run Signal Scanner to find new opportunities
    Go to Signal Scanner (localhost:3033). Set your lookback window (e.g. 72 hours) and run a scan. The scanner returns a list of signals — fund raises, new portfolio additions, job postings, and executive moves that match your profile.
  3. Score the opportunity
    Take an interesting signal to the Scorer (localhost:3038). Paste the company name and any context. The scorer returns a 0–100 fit score against your target profile. Focus on scores above 65.
  4. Research the company or fund
    For high-scoring opportunities, go to Research Hub (localhost:3035). Enter the company or fund name. The agent generates a deep brief including investment thesis, portfolio companies, recent news, and fit assessment. Save the JSON output — you will paste it into the Outreach Drafter as context.
  5. Find the right contact
    Go to Contact Research (localhost:3036). Enter the company name and your preferred contact type (Talent Partner, Operating Partner, or Managing Partner). The agent searches for the right person and verifies their email via FullEnrich. Copy the contact details.
  6. Draft the outreach email
    Go to Outreach Drafter (localhost:3037). Fill in Name, Email, Title, Company, Campaign Type, and paste the research JSON into the Research Context field. Click Generate Draft. Review the subject and body. Use the Rewrite buttons to adjust tone, proof points, or the ask. When satisfied, click Save Draft.
  7. Review and send from Gmail
    Open Gmail Drafts. Every draft created by the system appears here for your review. Read it fresh, make any final edits, and send. Never send without reading it yourself.
  8. Log the contact in Attio
    Email Scan (localhost:3034) will detect the sent email and create an Attio record or update the existing one. Set the next follow-up date in Attio at the time of sending.

B. Direct Outreach (you already have a contact)

  1. Go straight to Outreach Drafter
    If you already know who you are emailing and have done your own research, skip straight to Agent 5 (localhost:3037). Fill in the contact details and optionally add context in the research field.
  2. Generate, review, save
    Generate the draft, review carefully, save to Gmail Drafts, then send from Gmail.

C. Managing Replies

  1. Run Email Scan daily
    Go to Email Scan (localhost:3034) and run a scan with your preferred lookback (24 or 48 hours). The agent reads your inbox and surfaces replies, OOO messages, and bounces.
  2. Review Attio tasks created
    For each meaningful reply, Email Scan creates an Attio task with the suggested next action and a due date. Check Attio each morning and work through the task list.
  3. Draft follow-up replies
    Use Outreach Drafter for follow-up replies as well as cold outreach. Paste the prior email thread into the Research Context field to give the agent the full conversation history.

POSITIONING.md — The Source of Truth

Every draft Agent 5 generates is grounded in POSITIONING.md. This file contains your proof points, tone rules, fund-specific angles, what to avoid, and your call to action preference.

The file lives at: Track8 context/track-8/agent5-outreach-drafter/POSITIONING.md

If a draft is off-brand or missing key context, the fix is almost always in POSITIONING.md — not in the prompt. Update the file and regenerate.

Key sections in POSITIONING.md

  • Proof Points — arago must always appear first. Metrics must be exact.
  • Tone Guidance — no em-dashes, peer-to-peer register, never open with "I am reaching out to"
  • Fund-Specific Angles — tailored angle per fund (Montagu, Verdane, Creandum, Faraday, Almato)
  • What Not to Include — salary expectations, ALOMA revenue, technical stack names
  • Call to Action — always "30-minute call to explore fit"

You can also edit POSITIONING.md via the Positioning tab in the Outreach Drafter UI at localhost:3037.

Absolute Rules

  • Drafts onlyAll email output goes to Gmail Drafts. Nothing is ever sent automatically. You send every email.
  • arago firstarago always appears before ALOMA in any proof point list. Never swap the order.
  • No em-dashesNo em-dashes anywhere in outreach copy. Use commas or full stops instead.
  • Under 150 wordsCold outreach must be under 150 words. Warm contacts can go to 250.
  • Attio onlyOnly Agent 6 (Email Scan) writes to Attio. No other agent modifies CRM records.
  • Haiku onlyClassification tasks use claude-haiku-4-5. Never use claude-opus-4-6 in automation — too slow and expensive.
  • No salaryNever include salary expectations or relocation availability in outreach unless explicitly asked.

Tips and Gotchas

  • If positioningLoaded: false appears in a health check, POSITIONING.md is missing or the path in .env is wrong. Check the file exists at the correct path and restart Agent 5.
  • Research context passed to Agent 5 as JSON significantly improves draft quality. Always run Agent 2 first for warm contacts.
  • The Scorer is most useful for comparing multiple opportunities. Don't use it as a hard filter — a score of 55 with a strong personal connection beats a score of 80 with no warm intro.
  • FullEnrich has a monthly credit limit. Use Contact Research only when you have a strong candidate — don't enrich everyone in a list speculatively.
  • Email Scan should be run before each outreach session, not just reactively. Replies that sit unread for more than 48 hours lose momentum.
  • If an agent shows a red dot in the dashboard sidebar, check its log: cat /tmp/track8-agent[N].log
  • All agents restart cleanly with: claude-auto launch from the terminal. This is always the fastest way to get back to a known good state.

Podcast Monitor Port 3040

RSS and YouTube polling, Whisper transcription, and Claude summaries with topic tags.

Workflow

  1. Queue
    New episodes are polled from RSS and YouTube feeds and added to the queue automatically.
  2. Summarise
    Each episode is transcribed via Whisper and summarised by Claude into key takeaways.
  3. Topic tags
    Claude assigns topic tags to each summary for navigation and filtering.
  4. Timestamp links
    Deep summary sections are linked to timestamps in the original audio for quick reference.

Tags

Tags are navigation aids, not categories. They help you find relevant segments across episodes — not classify the episode as a whole.

Key endpoints

  • GET /api/episodesList all episodes with summaries
  • POST /api/episodes/:id/summariseTrigger summarisation for a specific episode
  • POST /api/summaries/:id/sectionGenerate a deep summary section with timestamps
  • POST /api/feedsAdd an RSS feed
  • POST /api/feeds/youtubeAdd a YouTube channel feed
  • GET /api/tagsList all topic tags

Settings

Polling frequency, feed list, and transcription model are configured via the Settings page or directly in the podcast-monitor .env file.

Deep summaries need transcripts: The /api/summaries/:id/section endpoint requires a completed transcript. Run summarisation first, then request deep sections with timestamps.
